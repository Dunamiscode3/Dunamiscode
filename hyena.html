There are two species of hyenas in Kenyaâ€”the striped hyena and the spotted hyena. They can be found throughout Africa, in all habitats except rainforests. The spotted hyena (also called the laughing hyena) is the largest hyena species, with the females being larger than the males. Their coats are patterned with dark spots. These hyenas are social animals, living in clans led by dominant females. They are scavengers but are also capable hunters, working together to take down large prey. Striped hyenas are smaller and more elusive than their spotted counterparts. They have striped coats, bushy tails, and a more solitary nature. They are mostly scavengers, feeding on carrion and bones. Both species play an important role in their ecosystems as scavengers and hunters, helping to clean up and balance the environment. However, they face threats such as habitat loss, hunting, and conflict with humans. The IUCN lists the spotted hyena as least concern but the striped hyena as near-threatened.
